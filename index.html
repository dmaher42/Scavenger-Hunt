Common Misinformation Techniques

False Attribution: Quoting fake or unnamed sources.
Appeal to Emotion: Using language that stirs strong feelings.
Lack of Evidence: Making claims without proof or data.
Visual Manipulation: Misleading images or edited photos.
Fear Appeal: Scaring readers to get attention.
Anecdotal Evidence: Using personal stories as proof.
Cherry Picking: Only showing one side or ignoring facts.
Loaded Language: Dramatic or emotional words to sway opinion.
Questionable Source: Information from blogs or rumors.
Framing Bias: Telling the story to support one point of view.
Emotive Language: Words meant to trigger emotion, not facts.
Confirmation Bias: Only showing info that supports a belief.
